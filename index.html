Functions: Linear vs Quadratic

Discover how straight lines and curves behave differently in mathematics

📏 Linear Functions

Create straight lines that never curve

y = mx + b

• Same change every step
• Constant slope everywhere
• Graph is always straight

📈 Quadratic Functions

Form U-shaped curves called parabolas

y = ax² + bx + c

• Rate of change varies
• Has a turning point
• Creates symmetric curves

💡 Key Insights

Two Ways to Think About Functions:

1. As a Machine: Put in x, get out y. Linear functions add/multiply by fixed amounts. Quadratic functions square the input.

2. As a Pattern: Linear patterns grow steadily. Quadratic patterns accelerate - they grow slowly then quickly (or vice versa).

Real World Examples:

Linear: Distance at constant speed, weekly savings, hourly wages

Quadratic: Area of a square, falling objects, profit optimization
